Fed Holds Rates Steady, Signals Two Cuts Possible in 2025
Region US · Texas
Relevance ●●●●●
Score 14
Washington D.C. — The Federal Open Market Committee voted unanimously on Wednesday to hold the federal funds rate at 5.25–5.5%, keeping borrowing costs at a 23-year high as policymakers await further evidence that inflation is returning to target.

Fed Chair Jerome Powell signaled cautious optimism, noting that the disinflation process is ongoing, though the path back to 2% remains uncertain. Markets had priced in a slim chance of a cut at this meeting.

The updated dot plot projects two quarter-point reductions in 2025, down from the four cuts penciled in at the September meeting — a hawkish revision that sent Treasury yields higher.

Impact for Texas businesses: Commercial lending rates tied to the prime rate remain elevated, keeping pressure on small business credit in Austin's expanding tech corridor.
